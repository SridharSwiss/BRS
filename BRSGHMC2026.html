Messenger & Location Playbook

The execution layer beneath the GHMC content strategy: who delivers each message, and where it lands. Right voice, right zone, right issue — that is how a city campaign is actually won.

Read this first — the geography is moving. GHMC has historically been 6 zones, 30 circles, 150 divisions. A 2025 delimitation has pushed wards toward 300, and there is a live proposal to split the city into three corporations (GHMC core, Cyberabad for the IT west, Malkajgiri for the east). This plan builds on the familiar 6-zone map; 🔴 lock final boundaries to the Election Commission's notification before printing division-level material. The restructuring is also a potential issue itself — see the closing note.
Part A

The Messenger Playbook

One rule above all: every messenger owns a lane. When the brand voice does pothole content or a local candidate does macro-economics, both lose credibility. Match the voice to the message and the audience.

KTR — the lead voice

Brand & air war
Owns
Brand Hyderabad, the IT/jobs story, the development record, the top-line attack, and the news cycle. He is the "we built this" argument.
Best audiences
IT/young professionals, middle class, media, opinion-shapers.
Channels
X (his strongest weapon), YouTube, marquee rallies, press conferences, English/Telugu interviews.
Tone
Confident, data-led, statesman-with-a-scalpel — pride upfront, sharp on contrast, never petty.
Signature content
Threads dropping verified data (IT exports, jobs); on-site reels at BRS-built infrastructure; rapid sourced rebuttals.
DO
  • Lead the IT-corridor & "Brand Hyderabad" narrative citywide
  • Own debates with sourced numbers from the briefs
  • Set the day's agenda; let others amplify locally
DON'T
  • Get pulled into ward-level pothole minutiae — delegate it
  • Trade personal insults or communal bait
  • Make any claim not in the fact-base

KCR — the patriarch

Emotional anchor · selective
Owns
Legacy, Telangana & Hyderabad pride, the big emotional set-piece. Scarcity is the strategy — a few high-impact appearances, not daily presence.
Best audiences
The committed base, older voters, sentiment/turnout.
Channels
Headline rallies, a small number of long-form addresses, archival "founder of modern Hyderabad" content.
Tone
Elder statesman; sweeping, emotional, legacy-framed.
Signature content
One or two peak-phase rallies; "the city he dreamed and built" film for the base.
DO
  • Deploy at peak for maximum turnout effect
  • Keep it legacy + vision, above the daily fray
DON'T
  • Over-expose — frequency erodes the impact
  • Use for rebuttals or hyperlocal detail

Division candidates & sitting corporators

Ground war · hyperlocal
Owns
The "Fix My Basti" pillar. Ward-level credibility — the drain, the road, the water line on your street. The single most decisive messenger group in a municipal poll.
Best audiences
Everyone in their division — especially homeowners, basti residents, women, traders.
Channels
WhatsApp broadcast lists, Facebook lives, door-to-door, corner meetings, local posters.
Tone
Accessible, accountable, "I live here too" — name the exact problem and the exact fix.
Signature content
Division "report cards" (built vs. stalled), walk-and-talk reels at the local problem spot, pledge drives.
DO
  • Speak only to issues you can name street-by-street
  • Localise every central message to your ward
  • Be the face of the Home-Protection Pledge locally
DON'T
  • Drift into state/national macro arguments
  • Promise what GHMC can't deliver — stay credible

Women leaders & corporators

Trust & kitchen-table issues
Owns
Water, neighbourhood health (Basti Dawakhana), safety, and the human face of demolition trauma. Carries the "dignity" theme most credibly.
Best audiences
Women across every segment; basti and middle-class households.
Channels
Facebook, WhatsApp, door-to-door, mahila/self-help-group meetings.
Tone
Empathetic, protective, practical.
Signature content
Testimony alongside (consented) HYDRAA-affected women; "water & clinics at your door" explainers.
DO
  • Centre real women's lived experience
  • Keep demolition stories dignified and consented
DON'T
  • Exploit victims for spectacle
  • Reduce women voters to a single "scheme" pitch

Youth & student leaders

Energy & the campus front
Owns
Kancha Gachibowli / environment, jobs & the IT slowdown, free expression. The moral-urgency voice.
Best audiences
Students, first-time voters, the IT corridor.
Channels
Instagram (Reels), YouTube, X, campuses.
Tone
Energetic, idealistic, fluent in platform-native formats and memes.
Signature content
"They sent bulldozers to your campus" explainers; jobs-decline reels; green-Hyderabad content.
DO
  • Lead with the verified SC observations on the forest
  • Speak the platform's native language
DON'T
  • Overclaim or meme past the facts
  • Pick communal fights to chase engagement

Real voices — victims, RWAs, IT workers, traders

The most persuasive of all
Owns
Authentic testimony — the demolition victim with valid papers, the RWA president, the laid-off techie, the Begum Bazaar trader. Surrogates outperform politicians on trust.
Best audiences
Persuadable middle, fence-sitters, every segment.
Channels
Every channel — these clips travel furthest.
Tone
Unscripted, real, first-person.
Signature content
Short consented testimonials; RWA roundtables; "a day in the stalled city" worker diaries.
DO
  • Get informed, documented consent every time
  • Verify each person's claim before publishing
DON'T
  • Stage or script "real" testimony
  • Expose vulnerable people to backlash without their understanding

Spokespersons & verified creators

Rebuttal & reach
Spokespersons
Own TV panels and the fact-check / rapid-rebuttal function. Disciplined, sourced, calm. They protect the campaign from being fact-checked by keeping our claims clean.
Influencers / creators
Hyderabad civic pages, meme accounts, RWA & IT voices — seed pride and civic content natively. All paid/collab content disclosed.
Tone
Spokespersons: authoritative. Creators: native to the platform.

Part B

The Location Playbook

The six GHMC zones, each with its own character, live issues, lead pillar, best messenger and ready content angles. 🟡 validate each issue with the local corporator before publishing.

Serilingampally Zone — the IT corridor

Gachibowli · Madhapur · Kondapur · Nanakramguda · Financial District · Kokapet · Manikonda · Narsingi  ·  affluent + IT-migrant, high-turnout-persuadable

Live issues: crippling traffic, tech-park flooding, Kancha Gachibowli (HCU) forest, HYDRAA demolitions in lake-belt layouts, the IT-jobs slowdown.

Lead pillar: Pride + Stalled City + Bulldozer (lake-belt). Lead messenger: KTR & youth/student leaders. Channels: Instagram, X, YouTube.

Hook 1 — KTR reel on a BRS-built flyover: "We made this India's IT capital. Two years on — gridlock, a frozen jobs market, and bulldozers in the campus forest next door."
Hook 2 — Student voice + verified SC line ("restore the forest or send officials to jail") over Kancha Gachibowli footage.

Kukatpally Zone — the dense west

Kukatpally · KPHB · Miyapur · Moosapet · Qutbullapur · Gajularamaram  ·  working/middle class, large settler population

Live issues: drinking-water reliability, traffic chokepoints, drainage/flooding, metro connectivity, fast unplanned growth.

Lead pillar: Fix My Basti + Pride (Metro). Lead messenger: division candidates & women leaders. Channels: WhatsApp, Facebook, ground.

Hook 1 — Corporator walk-through: "Here's the drain we cleared, here's the one they let silt up. Which Hyderabad do you want?"
Hook 2 — Women-led "water at your door" content contrasting supply now vs. the BRS build-out.

LB Nagar Zone — the growing east

LB Nagar · Dilsukhnagar · Saroornagar · Uppal · Hayathnagar · Nagole · Kapra  ·  aspirational middle class

Live issues: SRDP flyovers (a flagship BRS build here), drainage, potholes, traffic, civic upkeep.

Lead pillar: Pride + Fix My Basti. Lead messenger: candidates + KTR (infra). Channels: Facebook, WhatsApp, YouTube.

Hook 1 — "The LB Nagar flyover that ended your daily jam — built by BRS. What's been built for you since?"
Hook 2 — Before/after pothole & drainage report card, division by division.

Khairatabad Zone — the central mix

Khairatabad · Jubilee Hills · Yousufguda · Mehdipatnam · Karwan · Goshamahal (Begum Bazaar)  ·  affluent + dense central + trader belt

Live issues: HYDRAA demolitions (lake/farmhouse belt around Jubilee Hills), traffic, central-city water & sanitation, trader anxiety in Begum Bazaar.

Lead pillar: Bulldozer Govt + Fix My Basti. Lead messenger: real voices (traders, RWAs) + candidates. Channels: WhatsApp groups, Facebook, X.

Hook 1 — Begum Bazaar trader: "A city under the bulldozer is a market with no customers."
Hook 2 — Homeowner with valid papers + court stay, telling their HYDRAA story (consented).

Secunderabad Zone — the settled north

Secunderabad · Begumpet · Marredpally · Musheerabad · Amberpet · Malkajgiri · Alwal  ·  older twin-city, settled middle class, cantonment fringe

Live issues: ageing roads & drainage, cantonment-area civic gaps, traffic, parking.

Lead pillar: Fix My Basti + Pride. Lead messenger: candidates + women leaders. Channels: Facebook, WhatsApp, ground.

Hook 1 — "Old Secunderabad deserves new infrastructure — here's our ward plan vs. two years of neglect."
Hook 2 — Local clinic/Basti Dawakhana spotlight from a women leader.

Charminar Zone — the Old City

Charminar · Falaknuma · Chandrayangutta · Yakutpura · Malakpet · Santoshnagar  ·  dense, historic, AIMIM stronghold

Live issues: chronic civic neglect — sanitation, drainage, water, narrow-lane mobility, heritage upkeep.

Lead pillar: Fix My Basti (civic delivery only). Lead messenger: local candidates & real residents, in Telugu/Urdu. Channels: ground, WhatsApp, vernacular video.

Discipline: this is AIMIM turf — compete strictly on civic services and dignity. Keep it scrupulously non-communal; pick targeted winnable pockets rather than carpet-spending here.

Hook — Resident-led "our basti's broken drains and water" civic content, with a concrete fix pledge — no religious framing, ever.

Cross-cutting — the Musi belt

Riverfront stretches across Old City & central zones  ·  displaced & demolition-affected families

Live issue: riverfront-project displacement, demolitions, unmet rehab promises. Pillar: Bulldozer Govt. Messenger: real voices + women leaders. This belt produces the campaign's most powerful testimony — handle with consent and care.

Strategic watch — the GHMC restructuring

The move to redraw wards (toward 300) and possibly split the city into three corporations is both an operational risk (boundaries and the very units you're organising around may shift) and a potential message. If it advances, a disciplined, factual line to test: "Hyderabad is one city — don't carve it up for convenience; fix it." 🔴 Do not assert motive as fact — confirm the official rationale and status first, and let residents' disruption, not speculation, carry the argument.

Guardrails still apply, everywhere. Every claim sourced to the briefs and primary records; real quotes only (the Hitler remark is usable precisely because it's on record — never doctored media); consent for every victim; strictly non-communal framing (it's also the bigger tent); Model Code compliance; disclose paid/influencer content. A clean campaign is an un-discreditable campaign.
