BRS for GHMC 2026
Content & Communications Strategy

A shareable-first content plan to defend Brand Hyderabad, turn HYDRAA fear and the city's stall into votes, and win the bread-and-butter civic argument division by division.

00Bottom line

The one-sentence strategy

"We built this city - they're bulldozing it." Every piece of content should ladder up to one of two emotions: pride in what BRS built for Hyderabad, or fear/anger at what Congress is doing to it — and then land on the one civic problem in front of that voter, in their division.

GHMC is a municipal election, not a state one. That changes everything: voters here decide on garbage, drains, water, traffic and demolitions far more than on GSDP. So this strategy pairs the big emotional contrast (record vs. bulldozer) with a relentless hyperlocal layer, content tailored to each of the 150 divisions' actual problems.

Confidence tags used below: 🟢 high (sourced/verified) · 🟡 medium (directional) · 🔴 assumption (validate locally).

02The battleground

GHMC has 150 divisions across the city. In the last election the field split four ways.. BRS, BJP, AIMIM and a marginal Congress. The dynamics have now inverted: Congress holds the state (incumbency to defend and attack), BJP is pushing hard on a Hindutva-plus-anti-incumbency line, and AIMIM anchors the Old City. 🟡

  • vs Congress : attack the record: HYDRAA, IT/jobs decline, broken guarantees, Kancha Gachibowli. This is our main front.
  • vs BJP : compete on development credibility and "outsiders don't know this city"; avoid getting dragged onto purely communal terrain.
  • vs AIMIM : Old City is largely theirs; focus our energy on the IT corridor, West, East and peripheral growth belts where seats are winnable.

The opening: Congress is governing the city through demolition and uncertainty. That hands BRS a unifying, non-communal, pocket-and-home issue that cuts across caste and religion — the safety of your home and the future of your job.

04Content pillars

Five pillars. Every post belongs to one. Rough mix: 30% Pride · 30% Contrast · 25% Hyperlocal · 15% Vision.

Pillar 1 — Garv / Pride: "The City We Built" 30%

Why: pride is our moat. It reminds voters the good things they use daily are BRS-made — and frames Congress as a custodian, not a creator.

Clusters:
  • IT capital — exports ₹57k cr → ₹2.41L cr, 9 lakh jobs, Apple/Google/Amazon
  • The roads — SRDP flyovers, Durgam Cheruvu, the Metro
  • Homes & clinics — 2BHK, Basti Dawakhana, Mission Bhagiratha
  • Brand Hyderabad — global recognition, lakes, festivals

Pillar 2 — Bhay / The Bulldozer Govt 30%

Why: our sharpest emotional weapon. HYDRAA touches every homeowner; the Hitler remark hands us the "mindset" frame.

Clusters:
  • HYDRAA victim testimony (verified, consented) — homes razed, court stays ignored
  • Kancha Gachibowli — SC's "restore forest or jail officials"
  • Lagacharla — midnight raids, internet shutdown
  • The "Hitler" mindset — the CM's own words, condemned across parties

Pillar 3 — Rozmarra / "Fix My Basti" 25%

Why: GHMC is won on civic basics. This pillar is hyperlocal, division-by-division, and never goes stale.

Clusters:
  • Drains & monsoon flooding (the SNDP that was shelved)
  • Garbage & sanitation; potholes & roads
  • Drinking water & sewage; streetlights
  • Traffic & parking; stray dogs; property tax

Pillar 4 — Giravat / "The Stalled City" part of Contrast

Why: the data spine. Turns "vibes" of decline into hard, sourced numbers — strongest with IT/middle-class voters.

Clusters:
  • IT jobs down to ⅓; export growth halved
  • Real estate frozen (sales −23%, supply −38%)
  • SRDP/SNDP stalled; broken 6-guarantees scorecard

Pillar 5 — Bhavishya / "Hyderabad for All" 15%

Why: pride and fear need a forward promise to convert. This pillar carries the manifesto and community-specific outreach — and keeps the campaign positive enough to avoid pure-negativity fatigue.

Clusters: GHMC manifesto pledges (flood-free monsoon, IT-corridor mobility, home-protection guarantee, free clinics) · women's safety & water · youth/jobs · traders/MSME revival · Old City civic dignity · communal harmony as Hyderabad's brand.

08Signature campaigns & hashtags

  • #HyderabadWeBuilt / #BrandHyderabad — the pride engine; user-generated "my favourite BRS-built thing" posts.
  • #BulldozerSarkar / #SaveOurHomes — the HYDRAA fear campaign; pair with a real "Home-Protection Pledge" voters can sign.
  • #FixMyBasti / #MyDivisionMyVote — hyperlocal UGC: residents post their pothole/drain/water problem, tag the corporator.
  • #BringBackBrandHyderabad — the stalled-city/jobs frame for the IT corridor.
  • Signature asset: a "Home-Protection Pledge" + a division-level "Report Card" comparing what BRS built vs. what's stalled — both designed to be shared and signed.

09Rapid-response "issue desk"

A small always-on team that turns events into content within hours. 🟢 highest-ROI unit in the campaign.

  • HYDRAA watch — document every demolition (with consent), verify papers/court orders, publish within hours. This is the campaign's beating heart.
  • Monsoon desk — when it floods, be first with "this is the drain programme they shelved" + on-ground help.
  • Fact-check unit — rebut Congress claims with sourced counters; protect us from being fact-checked by keeping our own claims clean.
  • Gaffe capture — clip and contextualise opponents' own words (e.g. the Hitler remark) accurately, never doctored.

11Measurement & guardrails

Weekly scorecard

  • Share-of-voice vs BJP & Congress, per issue · sentiment in HYDRAA-affected wards
  • Reach & engagement by persona · video completion rates · hashtag trends
  • WhatsApp list growth · division-kit usage · volunteer sign-ups · pledge signatures
  • Ground-event turnout · earned-media pickups · rapid-response time
Non-negotiable guardrails — these protect the campaign as much as the public
  • Every claim sourced. Tie content back to the briefs and primary records (court orders, CAG, official IT data). A single fabricated number can sink a whole narrative.
  • Real quotes only. Use opponents' actual words (e.g. the Hitler remark, on record) — never doctored audio, video or invented quotes. That's both wrong and legally dangerous.
  • Consent for victims. Real people's stories only with informed consent; protect the vulnerable.
  • No communal or hate content. Keep the fight on homes, jobs and civic services — a non-communal frame is also the bigger tent, and it's where BRS wins.
  • Respect the Model Code of Conduct and platform rules; disclose paid/influencer content.

These aren't just ethics — a clean, sourced campaign is a campaign that can't be discredited mid-cycle. Accuracy is the strategy.

Fact-base for all content: the three companion briefs (neutral comparison, state-level BRS case, GHMC city case) and their underlying sources — CAG reports, Supreme Court / Telangana High Court orders, official Telangana IT data, Knight Frank/Anarock, and mainstream press. Validate every hyperlocal claim with the division's corporator before publishing.
